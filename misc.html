Misc

A running list of things that help me think and learn: mental models I try to use, podcasts I'm listening to, and websites I keep coming back to. If you know of something great that belongs here, feel free to let me know on X (twitter) or shoot me an email at sujayshahare@aol.com.

(last updated July 20th 2025)

Mental Models

Systems Thinking - seeing the world in terms of interconnected feedback loops rather than simple cause-and-effect chains. It's about understanding that an action in one part of a system can have delayed or non-obvious effects elsewhere.

First Principles Thinking - the practice of breaking down a problem to its most fundamental, undeniable truths and reasoning up from there. It's the antidote to reasoning by analogy or just following the crowd.

Probabilistic Thinking - viewing the world in shades of confidence, not black-and-white certainties. It involves thinking about the future as a range of possible outcomes and updating your beliefs as new evidence comes in (Bayesian updating).


Podcasts

Dwarkesh Podcast - Dwarkesh Patel - Dwarkesh conducts incredibly in-depth interviews with scientists, founders, and intellectuals. He gets right to the core of their ideas.

How to Take Over the World - Ben Wilson - Deep-dive biographies on history's most ambitious figures, from Julius Caesar to the Edison. It dissects their psychology, strategy, and tactics to understand how they achieved greatness.


Interesting Websites

Our World in Data - An indispensable resource for understanding the world through data, not headlines. It presents clear, well-researched charts and explanations on everything from global health to economic progress.

Hume Texts Online - Direct, free access to the complete works of the philosopher David Hume. An essential resource for anyone wanting to engage with his ideas on empiricism and skepticism directly from the primary source.

The Starter Guide to SF for Founders - A practical guide for founders navigating the San Francisco tech scene. It answers the tactical questions about fundraising, networking, and living in the epicentre of the startup world.

Your Life in Weeks - A simple visualization of your life in weeks.

Principles - Ray Dalio - An interactive resource based on Ray Dalio's framework for systematic decision-making. It goes beyond the book with tools, assessments, and case studies to help you codify your own principles for life and work.
